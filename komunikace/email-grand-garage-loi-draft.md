Dear [Name],

I hope this finds you well. My name is Ondřej Kašpárek, co-founder of Make More, a creative education and maker community organization based in the Czech Republic.

I'm reaching out because we are applying for a grant under the **(Perma)kulturní Open Call 2026**, a programme of **České Budějovice – European Capital of Culture 2028**, and we would like to invite Grand Garage to become our key international partner.

The formal grant applicant is our local partner **JVTP — Jihočeský vědeckotechnologický park** (South Bohemian Science and Technology Park), a public research and innovation institution based in České Budějovice. The grant programme requires the applicant to be a legal entity registered in the South Bohemian region, and JVTP fulfils this requirement — they also bring institutional capacity, infrastructure, and a Design Lab that forms the second pillar of the project. **Make More is the lead content and implementation partner**, driving the educational programme, the community model, and the day-to-day operations of the makerspace. In practice, we are co-designing and co-running the project together.

**The project: Make & Design Lab**

We are building a **pop-up makerspace in České Budějovice** — a city of 100,000 in South Bohemia that will be the European Capital of Culture in 2028. The project runs as a pilot in 2028, with a preparation year in 2027.

The core idea is straightforward: the South Bohemia region has a strong industrial base (Bosch, Engel, and others are headquartered here), excellent universities, and a generation of young people who deserve hands-on access to the tools and skills of the future — but there is no physical place where that can happen. **Make & Design Lab is built to fill that gap.**

The programme model:
- **Mornings:** School classes from primary and secondary schools — hands-on labs in digital fabrication, design, traditional and digital craft (~5,500 visits/year)
- **Evenings & weekends:** Open community platform — workshops, lectures, repair cafés, events for the wider public
- **Throughout:** Local industry and institutions (universities, research institutes, companies) as co-creators of the programme, not sponsors

**Why Grand Garage?**

When we look for inspiration and a mentor for this project, Grand Garage is the obvious reference. What you have built in Tabakfabrik Linz — a makerspace rooted in the conviction that industry, education and community belong together, housed in a repurposed industrial space, with TGW's long-term commitment as proof that this model is sustainable — is exactly what we are trying to bring to Budějovice.

We are particularly inspired by:
- The way Grand Garage connects industrial partners with educational programmes for youth, rather than keeping those worlds separate
- Your **Global Maker Education** initiative and the hands-on approach to addressing real-world challenges through making
- The community model that brings together students, start-ups, artists, researchers and companies in one shared space
- And honestly: the questions you must have had to navigate — building political will, securing long-term commitment, making the model work financially beyond the first grant

**What we are asking for**

We would value Grand Garage's involvement in two phases:

**2027 — Mentorship during preparation:**
As we design the curriculum, operational model, and community strategy for the pop-up makerspace, we would love to draw on your experience. This could take the form of 1–2 consultation sessions (online or in person), a visit to Linz for our team, and/or a short visit from your side to Budějovice as we set up. The goal is to learn from what you've built and adapt it intelligently to our context.

**2028 — Guest presence during the programme year:**
We would love to invite one or two people from Grand Garage to Budějovice — either as a guest lecturer for a workshop session, or as a speaker at one of our evening community events. We see this as a genuine exchange: we bring your know-how to a new audience, and you get to see what a fresh iteration of the model looks like.

The letter does not need to commit to specific deliverables at this stage — it simply confirms the partnership intent and your active role in the project (as a mentor and knowledge partner, not a supplier).

A short letter on Grand Garage letterhead, signed and dated, would be sufficient. We are happy to prepare a draft for you to review and adjust if that makes it easier.
